Who Can Apply?

The People's Bot is an experiment in using robots for the public good, to broaden access to physical events.

Too often, people with limited resources, kids, or long-distance travel get excluded from key meetings & opportunities.

To start, we're making the bot available to these three groups:

Students

High school, college, & grads: Get a People's Bot Scholarship

Media

Want to report on CHI? Apply for a Blogger/Journalism Fellowship

Anyone

We've put some time slots on Ebay, proceeds to fund student grants

Bots & The Public Good

Telepresent robots (watch a video) are often pitched as a technology to extend the influence of those who already have money and power.

We want to use robotic telepresence for the public good -- broadening access, supporting public interest reporting, and funding access initiatives.

Do you have ideas on using bots for the public good? Contact us.

The People's Bot In Action

The People's Bot is a project by Nathan Matias and Chelsea Barabas at the MIT Center for Civic Media and MIT Media Lab to rethink the use of robotic telepresence for the public good.

The People's Bot is testing telepresence for the public good, using bots to expand inclusion and representation of people typically excluded in society. Our project builds on prior work in robotic telepresence.

For press enquiries, contact Nathan Matias and Chelsea Barabas at thepeoplesbot@media.mit.edu. We are not affiliated with CHI or the ACM, so you should also contact the CHI press staff with any questions about CHI.

Upcoming Events

Apply To Attend CHI 2014, Monday April 28

The Computer Human Interaction conference is the world's most inspiring and flashy academic conference featuring beautiful and mind-blowing research. The conference features over 500 inspiring projects from universities around the world and will include a special 13-year retrospective on wearable computing and the history of Google Glass. It's also a place where students find out about jobs, find collaborators, and work out their future research.

Although we're not officially affiliated with CHI, we registered the bot to attend so we can offer scholarships, fellowships, and an eBay auction to attend the conference via a bot.

Our bot will follow all CHI rules, including rules about filming. If you win a scholarship, you will also receive bot training a few days in advance, so you can make the most of your experience.

If you're in Toronto, you may not need a bot to see the exhibits! On April 29 from 5:30pm to 7:30pm, CHI is opening its exhibits to the public for a $10 admission fee.

If you want to learn more about robotic telepresence, why not look up these great papers from CHI on video telepresence?

DEADLINE: 3pm ET on Monday, April 21st

Ebay Auctions: History of Google Glass Exhibit

Bid for a chance to visit CHI's exhibit on the history of Google Glass and wearable computing via telepresent robot. The winners of these two 30 minute slots will get to move around the exhibit, talk with attendees, and interact with people and research that have led to Google Glass. This remarkable interactive exhibit has been curated by Thad Starner, technical lead on Google Glass, and technology fashion designer Clint Zeagler.

Proceeds from the Ebay auction support our public and educational mission. All proceeds will be donated to the CHI student travel grants, funding access for students who can't afford key academic conferences.
